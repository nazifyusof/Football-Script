Fallen Giants

The first shot on target of the game was in the 29th minute. It was also the first shot on goal in the game for both sides. This speak volumes on how far both teams have fallen. The game ended in a 1-1 draw. Points shared, a suitable result for an abysmal game. Neither team deserved the full 3 points.

Both teams lacked the conviction to take control of the game. Manchester United’s forward line were largely ineffective. Their midfield lacked the intensity to split open the Arsenal side. Only a fast break, followed by a wonderful shot by Scott McTominay, were they able to obtain the lead. Marcus Rashford looked a whole different player in recent weeks, compared to the player who tore apart Chelsea in Gameweek 1. He looked unconfident and tired, fluffing big chances throughout the game.

The fact that Jesse Lingard gets the nod ahead of Mason Greenwood baffles me. Lingard has yet to score or assist in any Premier League games this calendar year and still manage to start ahead of Greenwood who has been performing really well each time he is on the pitch. Greenwood has 4 goals to his name with the senior squad, including the preseason friendlies. He’s always a threat when playing and should have started against Arsenal. United’s lack of experienced forwards was laid bare this time. Alexis Sanchez and Romelu Lukaku who left for Inter Milan in the summer would have definitely gave a boost to the squad, at least a little if not much.

Arsenal on the other side of the field were no different. Granit Xhaka was too slow, and the midfield was too reliant on the young Matteo Guendouzi to play the box-to-box role and control the midfield. The dynamic Lucas Torreira without a doubt has the ability to create chances but was kept quiet by McTominay all game. Arsenal’s lack of a commanding voice in their back-line has also left something for their manager, Unai Emery, to ponder. No one tracked McTominay in the movement which lead to the first goal of the game.

The addition of David Luiz was greeted with cheers in the summer, but he looked more of a liability in the game. Luiz almost gave United a penalty when he made a rash tackle, which completely misses the ball, at the edge of the box in the 90th minute. Nicolas Pepe seemed too eager to justify his £72 million price tag and wasted multiple clear-cut chances. Arsenal’s only goal came in the 58th minute when United’s left-back of the day, Axel Tuanzebe, mishit a back pass too strong that got to the foot of Bukayo Saka who’s through pass was slotted home by Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang. Tuanzebe had been solid all-game but his first mistake led to a goal for Arsenal.

Both Manchester United and Arsenal didn’t seemed to had any clear tactics in their approach to the game. United had more possession of the ball overall but didn’t really do anything with it. Too many safe side-passes never threatened the Arsenal ranks. United were content with moving the ball side to side and this style of play didn’t stretch the Arsenal players enough for United’s forwards to be able to make anything happen. Arsenal relied on their pacy forwards from the start but United’s defense perservered.

This game was one of the worst performance from both sides ever. It was full of sloppy passes, mistimed shots, mistimed tackles, poor pressing when off the ball, poor back-tracking when defending and poor ball retention. Both teams didn’t had any direction to how they wanted to play the game out. Most of the time after dribbling some distance, players looked lost and didn’t know how to proceed when they were rushed by defenders. A stark contrast to Manchester City and Liverpool who are well drilled in both attack and defense. The lack of quality in the Arsenal and Manchester United squads, as compared to the Liverpool and Manchester City squads are alarming.

A battle between United and Arsenal used to be the title decider, the game changer that would shape how a season would end. Nowadays it is really just a fight to remain relevant, in an era where Liverpool and City are the true elites. It is honestly sad to see how far these clubs who ruled the 2000s have fallen. The post-Ferguson era hasn’t been kind to United, while Arsenal has been toppled by their North London neighbors in recent years. United now lavish at 10th place and Arsenal at 4th. With how things are going, it wouldn’t be long until they are too much behind the league leaders both in terms of points and development.
